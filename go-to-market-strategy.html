1. ICP & Positioning

Dimension Detail

Company B2B, $5M–$50M revenue, 20–200 employees
Mindset Smart operator who built a real business — but AI is the first technology wave where they feel genuinely behind

Private equity operating partners looking to create value across portfolio companies by rolling out a repeatable AI strategy framework. This is the scale play — but it requires proof from the primary ICP first. Park it for now.

The Positioning Statement

Positioning

"The embedded AI strategy partner for companies too smart to wing it and too lean to staff it."

This positions against every alternative in one sentence:

  • vs. consultants: "We don't advise and leave — we build and stay."
  • vs. tool vendors: "We don't sell software — we design the system that makes your software work."
  • vs. hiring: "You get a senior team for less than one senior hire."

The Competitive Frame

Your ICP isn't comparing you to McKinsey. They're weighing three alternatives — and you beat all three:

Alternative Why They'll Consider It Why It Falls Short
Buy a tool (Salesforce Agentforce, generic AI platforms) Seems turnkey. Vendor does the selling for you. They've already been pitched Agentforce. Weren't impressed. Tools without strategy are expensive shelf-ware.
Hire a "Head of AI" Would give them internal ownership. $200K+ fully loaded. At SMB scale, this is a budget they don't have for a bet they can't de-risk.
Wing it (ChatGPT + hope) Free. No commitment. No architecture, no integration, no measurement. Experiments that never become systems.

The Objection Neutralizer

The most likely objection: "Have you done this before?"

Reframe

"We've run this exact motion — strategy blueprinting, technology implementation, change management, value creation — across dozens of Salesforce transformations. AI is a new technology, but the problem is the same: how do you design and implement a technology system that creates real business value? The technology changed. The discipline didn't."

This reframes expertise from "AI experience" to "transformation experience" — which is what actually matters. And it's true.

2. The Core Philosophy: Proof Through Utility

Traditional marketing for professional services follows a predictable loop: write blog posts, accumulate case studies, wait for SEO compounding, hope for inbound. That works — in year two. We don't have that luxury.

The alternative: replace proof of past performance with proof of present competence.

Instead of saying "look what we did for others," we say "look what we can do for you, right now." The interactive tool IS the proof. The strategy report a prospect receives IS the demonstration of expertise. No case studies required.

Principle

Every piece of marketing should do something for the prospect — not just say something about Olytic. Interactive utility content over static authority content.

Pillar 1: The Interactive Assessment

Full design detailed in Section 4. This is the centerpiece asset — the lead magnet, proof of competence, and sales qualification engine rolled into one.

Pillar 2: The O/G/O Framework as IP

Most firms at this stage don't have a named, repeatable methodology. Olytic does. The Operator / Governor / Optimizer framework needs its own dedicated page — not a blog post, a framework page. Think of how IDEO owns "Design Thinking" or how Salesforce built the V2MOM.

This is your credential when you have no case studies: "We don't have a portfolio of past clients. We have a methodology we've pressure-tested and a background that tells you we know how to apply it."

Pillar 3: Opinionated Content

Not "5 Tips for AI." The bar is: every piece should make the reader think "this person has actually done this work." Examples:

  • "Your AI pilot failed because nobody asked the revenue question first."
  • "The 'Head of AI' hire you can't afford wouldn't have solved your problem anyway."
  • "You don't have an AI problem. You have a systems problem."

6. GEO: AI Answer Engine Optimization

Traditional SEO is a 6–12 month compounding game. GEO is about being the source that AI assistants cite when your ICP asks questions. The dynamics are different:

Traditional SEO GEO (AI Answer Engine Optimization)
Optimize for Google's ranking algorithm Optimize for AI assistants selecting you as a source
Keyword density and backlinks matter most Specificity, authority, and structured answers matter most
Compete on domain authority Compete on answer quality
Requires 6–12 months to compound Can be indexed and cited much faster

GEO Content Strategy

Write 3–4 definitive long-form pages that answer the exact questions your ICP is asking AI assistants:

  • "How should a mid-market B2B company approach AI strategy?"
  • "What's the ROI of AI consulting for SMBs?"
  • "What should a company without a Head of AI do first?"
  • "How to evaluate AI readiness for a $20M B2B company"

Structure these with clear headings, specific numbers, and opinionated positions. AI assistants prefer specificity and structured data over generality. Be the most useful answer, not the most comprehensive one.

9. Strategic Takeaways & How AI's Trajectory Shapes Them

These aren't observations — they're load-bearing assumptions the strategy is built on. If any of them turn out to be wrong, the plan needs to change. Each one also has a directional implication for how AI's evolution will affect the window of opportunity.

1. The 2026 Inflection Point Is Real — and Time-Limited

The market shifted at the start of 2026 from "interesting noise" to "we can't wait." Buyers feel urgency but haven't committed to a vendor yet. That's the gap Olytic exists to step into.

AI Trajectory Implication

This window closes. As AI tools mature and established consulting firms develop repeatable AI practices, the "strategic vacuum" for SMBs will fill — either from above (enterprise firms moving downstream) or from below (AI tools becoming self-explanatory). The first-mover advantage is real, but it has a shelf life of 12–24 months. Speed of credibility-building matters as much as quality.

2. Interactive Utility Content Replaces Case Studies — For Now

The AI Strategy Architect tool demonstrates Olytic's quality of thinking before any money changes hands. A prospect who completes it has already experienced the product. No testimonials required.

AI Trajectory Implication

As AI-generated tools become commoditized, the tool itself stops being differentiation and the quality of the strategy behind the tool becomes the differentiator. Invest in the prompt engineering and scoring logic now — that intellectual property compounds. The UI is replaceable; the framework is not.

3. Buyers Are Skeptical of AI Without Judgment Behind It

The ICP isn't skeptical of AI anymore — they're skeptical of AI with no business strategy attached. Agentforce failed them not because it was AI, but because it was AI without a revenue thesis. The winning position: transformation expertise applied to AI, not AI expertise in isolation.

AI Trajectory Implication

As AI capabilities become table-stakes, the market will increasingly pay for strategic judgment rather than technical implementation. Olytic should resist the temptation to build technical capability for its own sake. The embedded strategy partner role becomes more valuable as AI tools become easier to deploy — because the question shifts from "can we implement this?" to "should we, and how?"

4. GEO Has a First-Mover Window That Won't Last

AI assistants are actively building their citation graphs right now. Specific, structured, opinionated content gets referenced in perpetuity. Generic content gets ignored. The firms that publish the right content in the next 6–12 months get embedded as default sources.

AI Trajectory Implication

This window is narrow. As AI search matures, citation graphs stabilize and new entrants face higher barriers to displacement — the same compounding dynamic as traditional SEO, but faster. The O/G/O framework as a named, citable methodology is a direct GEO asset. Name things. Define them. Publish them now.

5. "Transformation Experience" Outranks "AI Experience" — For Now

The market doesn't yet have a clear picture of what good AI implementation looks like. It does know what bad technology transformations look like. Olytic's Salesforce and RevOps background is a more reassuring credential than pure AI experience, because buyers have lived through CRM disasters and failed digital initiatives.

AI Trajectory Implication

This advantage erodes as the market develops its own AI case study library. In 18–24 months, "we've run dozens of Salesforce transformations" becomes less compelling as successful AI transformations accumulate at competitor firms. The credibility bridge from Salesforce to AI is a temporary asset. Use it aggressively now to win early clients, then replace it with Olytic's own AI transformation track record.

6. The PE Scale Play Requires Patience — and Preparation

The portfolio rollout model (one framework, deployed across multiple companies via a PE operating partner) is the legitimate scale play. But it requires a replicable, documented methodology — not a promise.

AI Trajectory Implication

PE firms are already beginning to systematize AI across portfolios. The operating partners who don't have a framework yet will be actively looking for one. Document the O/G/O methodology as if you're writing it for someone who has never met Olytic. That document becomes the pitch to a PE operating partner. The AI trajectory accelerates this timeline — PE urgency around AI is increasing, not decreasing.
