## Vision

Olytic is an **AI implementation and optimization platform** built on a simple observation: every company using AI tools has the same problem — the AI is only as good as the day it was set up. Olytic fixes that.

We build a self-improving layer on top of existing AI plugins. Every week, the Optimizer pulls analytics, CRM data, and competitive intelligence — and automatically surfaces strategic updates to the plugin and a prioritized content brief queue. The plugin gets smarter every Monday without anyone having to think about it.

Across all clients, Olytic is building a cross-company behavioral dataset that doesn't exist anywhere else — which we monetize as intelligence. Our competitors get commoditized. We get compounding.

## Philosophy

**"Amplification, not automation"** is not a tagline. It's an architectural principle. We aren't trying to replace humans with automation — we empower them through a closed loop: analysis and recommendations for improvement, based on outcomes and changes in the landscape, are automatically served up to people. But the human still gets to have agency.

The **O/G/O framework** (Operator / Governor / Optimizer) is how this philosophy becomes a system.

## Core Values

These values govern how Olytic operates internally and how we show up for clients. They are not aspirational — they describe how we actually work.

- **Clarity over noise.** We cut through complexity. If we can't explain something simply, we don't understand it yet. Every deliverable should make things clearer, not add to the fog.
- **Embedded partnership.** We're not vendors. We don't advise and leave. We're still here at meeting #20, and that changes how we work.
- **Amplification, not automation.** AI serves people, not the reverse. Humans stay in control at every decision point. We build systems that make people sharper — not systems that replace them.
- **Systems thinking.** We build loops, not one-offs. Every engagement has a feedback mechanism. Every recommendation connects to an outcome. We don't hand off and walk away.
- **Earned expertise.** We earn the right to be opinionated by doing the work. We take positions because we've thought them through — not because we're performing confidence.

**What's included:**

## Long-Term Vision: The Optimizer Platform

The Optimizer is the technology layer that separates Olytic from any freelance consultant. It's a weekly AI agent that runs against each client's data and makes their plugin smarter every Monday — automatically.

**Data sources the Optimizer analyzes:**
- Google Analytics 4 — page performance, engagement, conversions → identifies which content formats drive traffic
- Google Search Console — keyword rankings, CTR, impressions → surfaces keyword opportunities and positioning gaps
- CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce) — lead-to-opportunity rates, content attribution → connects content strategy to pipeline and revenue
- Competitive intelligence — web search for competitor content, positioning shifts → flags competitive moves and repositioning opportunities
- Plugin usage logs — how the marketer uses the plugin, what they override → identifies where the plugin is misaligned with real usage

**Output:** Two recommendation tracks — **Plugin Updates** (strategic changes to the plugin itself, sent to the client stakeholder for approval) and **Creator Briefs** (tactical content briefs for the marketing team, delivered as a prioritized queue every Monday morning).

## Go-to-Market Strategy: The Zero Case Study Playbook

*Source: Internal GTM Strategy Document, March 2026 v1.0*

Olytic's GTM strategy is called **The Zero Case Study Playbook** — a deliberate approach to winning our first client through interactive utility content instead of waiting for case studies, SEO compounding, or traditional inbound.

### Core Philosophy: Proof Through Utility

The guiding principle: **replace proof of past performance with proof of present competence.** Instead of saying "look what we did for others," we say "look what we can do for you, right now."

Every piece of marketing should **do** something for the prospect — not just **say** something about Olytic. Interactive utility content over static authority content.

### The Three Pillars

1. **The Interactive Assessment (AI Strategy Architect)** — an interactive tool that serves as lead magnet, proof of competence, and sales qualification engine in one. Prospects take an 8–12 question branching assessment, receive an AI-generated personalized strategy report, and immediately experience Olytic's quality of thinking. Meanwhile, Olytic gets a fully-qualified lead profile. The bridge to paid: "This is the 10-minute version. The full AI Strategy Blueprint goes deeper."

2. **The O/G/O Framework as Publishable IP** — a dedicated framework page (not a blog post) that positions the Operator / Governor / Optimizer methodology as a named, citable methodology. Think how IDEO owns "Design Thinking." This IS the credential when there are no case studies: "We don't have a portfolio of past clients. We have a methodology we've pressure-tested."

3. **Opinionated Content** — not "5 Tips for AI." Every piece should make the reader think "this person has actually done this work." Contrarian, specific, practical positions.

### The Objection Neutralizer

The most likely objection: **"Have you done this before?"**

The reframe: "We've run this exact motion — strategy blueprinting, technology implementation, change management, value creation — across dozens of Salesforce transformations. AI is a new technology, but the problem is the same: **how do you design and implement a technology system that creates real business value?** The technology changed. The discipline didn't."

This reframes expertise from "AI experience" to "transformation experience" — which is what actually matters.

### Channel Strategy

- **LinkedIn Organic (primary):** 4–5 short-form posts per week. Contrarian, specific, opinionated. Each ends with a soft CTA to the interactive tool. This outperforms paid LinkedIn at the current budget.
- **Google Ads ($500/mo):** 3–5 exact-match keywords pointing to the interactive tool landing page. Targets: "AI strategy assessment," "AI readiness for business," "AI consulting SMB." Expected: 7–12 qualified leads/month.
- **GEO (AI Answer Engine Optimization):** 3–4 definitive long-form pages answering the exact questions the ICP asks AI assistants. First-mover window of 6–12 months before citation graphs stabilize.
- **Warm Outreach (Day 25–30):** Personal outreach to 20 warm LinkedIn connections with the tool link. Not a pitch — "built this, thought you'd find it interesting."

## How This Informs Work

When producing any work product, consider:

- **Does this serve the current phase?** Right now, that means winning case study clients and building proof points. Generic content or long-horizon thinking doesn't help right now unless it directly supports near-term acquisition.
- **Does this demonstrate that we've done it ourselves?** The biggest differentiator is proof. We built our own plugins before selling to anyone. Surface this wherever possible.
- **Does this differentiate from freelance AI consultants?** Anyone can consult. The Optimizer and the data asset are what make us defensible. Keep that visible.
- **Is this consistent with the Claude Cowork partner angle?** Position as THE implementation specialist for Cowork, not a generic AI consultant.
- **Does this reflect our values?** Clarity, partnership, amplification, systems thinking, earned expertise — these should be visible in the work.
